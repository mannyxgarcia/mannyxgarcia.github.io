Popular Websites

Before social media became popular, people explored the Internet through search engines, personal websites, chat rooms, and online communities. Many of these websites introduced new ways to communicate, share information, and express creativity. While some have disappeared or changed dramatically, they helped build the foundation of today's web.

Yahoo!

Yahoo! was one of the most popular websites on the Internet. It served as both a search engine and a directory where users could browse categories such as news, sports, shopping, and entertainment. For many people, Yahoo! was their homepage every time they opened a web browser.

AOL Instant Messenger (AIM)

AIM allowed friends and family to chat with each other in real time. Users created screen names, customized away messages, and waited to hear the famous notification sound whenever a buddy signed online. Instant messaging quickly became one of the most popular ways to communicate over the Internet.

GeoCities

GeoCities gave anyone the opportunity to build a personal website without needing advanced programming knowledge. Many pages featured colorful backgrounds, animated GIFs, visitor counters, guestbooks, and flashing text. It became one of the most recognizable examples of early web design.

Napster

Napster changed the way people shared music online by allowing users to exchange MP3 files directly with one another. Although it became involved in major copyright lawsuits, it introduced many people to peer-to-peer file sharing and influenced future online music services.

MySpace

Launched in the early 2000s, MySpace became one of the first major social networking websites. Users could customize their profile pages with music, colors, images, and HTML, making every profile unique. It helped pave the way for modern social media platforms.

Fun Fact

A common dial-up speed was only 56 Kbps. Today, many home Internet connections are thousands of times faster.
